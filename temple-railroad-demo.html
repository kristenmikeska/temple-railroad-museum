Our Story

Temple Didn't Just Grow Alongside the Railroad.
It Was Born Because of It.

On June 29, 1881, the Gulf, Colorado & Santa Fe Railway held a barbecue on the Texas prairie and auctioned off plots of land. The city — named after Chief Engineer Bernard M. Temple — sprang up almost overnight. Within three years, it had 3,000 residents, three churches, a school, two banks, and two newspapers.

The railroad didn't just found Temple. It built the city's first hospital in 1891. It connected Central Texas to the nation. And when the historic Santa Fe Depot fell quiet in the 1990s, the people of Temple invested $4 million to bring it back.

The Temple Railroad & Heritage Museum, now housed in that fully restored 1909–1911 depot, holds one of the country's most significant archives of Santa Fe Railway history. This is not just a building. It is the original heartbeat of this city — and we intend to keep it beating.
