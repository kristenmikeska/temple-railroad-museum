Our Story

Temple Didn't Just Grow
Alongside the Railroad.
It Was Born Because of It.

On June 29, 1881, two major railways — the Gulf, Colorado & Santa Fe Railway and the Missouri-Kansas-Texas Railroad — converged at Temple Junction and auctioned off 157 business lots and 28 residential lots on the Texas prairie. The city was named in honor of Bernard Moore Temple, the Santa Fe's chief engineer.

The railroad didn't just found Temple. It built its first hospital in 1891. It connected Central Texas to the nation. And when the historic Santa Fe Depot fell quiet in the 1990s, the people of Temple invested $4 million to bring it back.
