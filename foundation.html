Who We Are

About the Foundation

The Railroad and Heritage Museum Foundation Board grew out of the original advisory board for the Museum when the City of Temple assumed full operational management in 2012. From the beginning, our mission has been clear: to administer the Fred Springer Endowment and to raise private funds for projects that are outside the scope of the museum's operating budget.

The museum does extraordinary work every day — maintaining exhibits, welcoming visitors, stewarding one of the most significant Santa Fe Railway archives in the country. The Foundation exists to make the extraordinary possible: the capital improvements, the landmark exhibitions, and the investments in the historic depot that no annual budget can carry alone.

We are actively growing. We are seeking new board members, expanding our Supporting Member program, and building the institutional foundation that this museum's next century deserves. If you believe in this history and want to do more than buy a ticket — this is where that begins.

Where Your Support Goes

What the Foundation Funds

01

Capital Projects

Major improvements to the historic 1911 Santa Fe Depot and museum facilities — structural preservation, accessibility upgrades, exhibit gallery renovations — that fall outside the museum's annual city budget.

02

Special Exhibitions

Landmark rotating exhibits, traveling collections, and signature programming that require investment beyond what the museum's operating funds can support. The exhibitions that make people drive from three counties away.

03

The Fred Springer Endowment

The Foundation's core permanent fund, generating ongoing income to support the museum in perpetuity. Gifts to the Endowment grow over time and provide a stable, lasting foundation for the museum's future.

Legacy Giving

The Fred Springer Endowment

The Fred Springer Endowment is the Foundation's permanent fund — the financial backbone that supports the museum in perpetuity. Unlike annual operating funds, the Endowment is invested over time, generating ongoing income that benefits the museum year after year, regardless of economic conditions or budget cycles.

The Endowment is named for Fred M. Springer, whose archives have contributed irreplaceable photographs and documents to the museum's collection — including the earliest known photographs of the 1911 Santa Fe Passenger Station. A gift to the Endowment honors that legacy and extends it forward.

The Foundation administers the Endowment directly. To discuss a gift — whether a one-time contribution, a recurring commitment, or a planned legacy gift — contact the Foundation Board.

Board Recruitment

Join the Foundation Board

The Foundation Board is actively seeking new members. We are a small, committed group with big ambitions for what this museum can become — and we need community leaders, professionals, and history advocates to help us get there.

Board service is a meaningful commitment to Temple's cultural future. Members help shape the Foundation's fundraising strategy, guide endowment stewardship, and bring the community connections that make private support possible.

We're looking for people who bring:

  • Passion for Temple's history and the museum's mission
  • Professional experience in finance, law, marketing, education, or philanthropy
  • Community ties and a network of potential supporters
  • Capacity for active, engaged board participation
  • Commitment to the Foundation's long-term growth

Board membership does not require prior nonprofit experience — it requires genuine commitment. If you've ever thought this museum deserves more, this is how you do something about it.

Tell us a little about yourself and why you'd like to join the board. The Foundation will be in touch to schedule a conversation.
